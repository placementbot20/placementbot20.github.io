Analyst

  • Aug 12, 2020
  • Placement Bot

Less than a month:

  1. Most companies have Business Analyst/ Risk and Financial Analyst/Consultants. Some of the Job Descriptions are given at Google Drive Link (2019). You can have a look to get an idea. (subject to change every year)
  2. Online tests will have a mix of aptitude, logical reasoning and sometimes 1-3 coding questions.
  3. Aptitude onIndiaBix, GfG and puzzles from InterviewBit are good.  Keep a comfortable time range for the first day, such as 20 questions in 40 mins. Try to reduce the time taken after every 4 tests. The aptitude questions should also be a mixed bag, after completing the time bound tests, look at the questions you got wrong or found difficult, see which bucket they fall into and solve at least 10 different varieties of questions from that bucket (non time bound) till you are comfortable with that type. It's all about speed vs accuracy.
  4. For interviews you need to see youtube videos for JAM sessions and how to answer Guesstimates or puzzles. This will give you a clear strategy and practice this because this is the first step of any round. No matter how good you are technically, if you don’t clear this round, you will be eliminated. Search online for the current affairs topics that can be asked in a Group Discussion (GD) so that you can have a few points ready beforehand 

    5. Guesstimates: There are quite a few guesstimates available online (on youtube too) that have overlapping themes, and follow a very common stream of thought. Make sure you know these in and out. It is very important to interact with the interviewer and think out loud. Ask questions to clarify your doubts. Drilling down on a problem is important. It is your approach that matters.

    Group Discussion: You may be given a topic to discuss, or a case study. 

    If you understand the topic well enough, you may start off the discussion. Speak confidently and clearly, speak to the point. Do not speak over other people, but be an active participant. If you see someone being spoken over, give them a chance to speak. 

    If it is a case study, take the given time to carefully read through the case. Work with your team members and give useful inputs. Start reading up on what is happening in the tech world, as most of these case studies are a mix of business and tech. Search for case studies on websites of top consulting firms, or on youtube to get a good idea. 

    Puzzles: GeeksForGeeks has a list of puzzles that are most commonly asked. Even if you don’t know how to code them out, you should be thorough with the logic. Think out loud, and explain the process as you solve the puzzle step by step.

    Resume: The interviewer will always focus on your resume. Be prepared to answer any question from your resume. It is highly recommended to tailor your resume to the job profile. For more Resume tips refer to the “Interview preparation” tips on the chatbot.

  5. IMPORTANT: Math puzzles: Mostly the interviewer asks readily-available puzzles on the internet and if you already know it just ACT LIKE YOU DON’T KNOW the solution. And build a thought process out-loud. Go from basics to Advanced thinking and then finally present the solution that you already know :) ( Search on google for links) 

    6. For example: http://www.crazyforcode.com/top-10-interview-puzzles/

  6. If you have less than a month, you can do basic coding LOGICS for interviews but this is completely optional. Mostly they ask basic questions from arrays, strings, searching and sorting algorithms. Sometimes they don’t even want you to code, but want the programming logic to be explained.

    7. NOTE: You don’t need to go very deep in coding and can learn basics just for interview and Online test purposes. Some online tests would not have coding questions. If the role is technical business analyst related then you will be asked coding more.

  7. (OPTIONAL): (If you want to learn basic coding): Recommended: InterviewBit or Leetcode (Easy tagged Top 100 interview questions) : Arrays, strings, binary search questions (can skip level 1 of interviewbit if you don’t have time, can read time-complexity of searching algorithms) (Can do corresponding topics from other platform if you are already following another platform) 

Process: Learn a topic → See syntax and one example question → try a new question from the same topic (Do one question from each bucket)→ Spend 5 minutes understanding the question → Try it on your own for at least 15-20mins → If not able to solve see solution (due to time constraint) → understand the solution and try to reduce the time each concurrent week.

About Placement Bot

The Placement Bot is an AI powered chatbot that provides job specific, time bound and personalized guidance for placements including tips on competitive coding, aptitude, logical reasoning, interviews, online tests and career advice for on-campus and off-campus placements.

The motive behind this voluntary initiative was to help engineering students prepare for placements in a more personalised way and eliminate the one-size-fit-all guidance provided all over the web.
